CREMA

Maintaining a home espresso machine is essential for consistent coffee quality and a long-lasting machine. After each use, you should remove and rinse the portafilter, knock out the used coffee puck, and wipe the steam wand to prevent milk buildup. Weekly, backflush the machine if it supports it to clean out coffee oils and residues from the group head. Periodically, usually every few months depending on water hardness, descale the machine to remove mineral deposits. Regular cleaning of removable parts like the drip tray, water tank, and baskets also helps keep the machine hygienic. Consistent maintenance ensures your espresso tastes fresh and prevents mechanical issues over time.
