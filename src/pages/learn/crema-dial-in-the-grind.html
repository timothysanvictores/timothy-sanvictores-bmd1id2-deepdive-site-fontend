Dialing in your grind means adjusting how fine or coarse your coffee is so the espresso flows at the right speed and tastes balanced. Start with a standard recipe (about 18g of coffee yielding 36g of espresso in 25–30 seconds) and only change the grind size at first. If the shot runs too fast and tastes sour, the grind is too coarse, so make it finer. If it runs too slow and tastes bitter, the grind is too fine, so make it coarser. Make small adjustments each time and test again until the shot flows evenly and tastes smooth.
