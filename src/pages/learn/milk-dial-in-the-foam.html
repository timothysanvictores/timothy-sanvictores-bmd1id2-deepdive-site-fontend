Several techniques affect how milk turns out when steaming, and mastering them is key to achieving smooth, velvety microfoam. The position of the steam wand matters: keeping it just below the milk’s surface while angled slightly creates a whirlpool that mixes the milk and foam evenly. The amount of air you introduce, called stretching, also impacts texture—a brief, gentle hiss at the surface adds tiny bubbles for silky microfoam, while too much air makes the milk frothy and bubbly. The swirling motion and how long you steam the milk determine consistency and temperature, with overheating leading to scorched milk and underheating producing thin foam. Finally, tapping and swirling the pitcher after steaming helps break up larger bubbles and integrates the foam, resulting in a glossy, uniform texture ready for pouring.
